School Campus

Auditorium

The auditorium has two portions-the lower & upper auditorium.The lower auditorium is accessible through the first floor while the upper auditorium is accessible from the second floor.The lower auditorum is mostly used for dance classes,performances,
rehersals and daily assemblies.

Laboratory

There are three labs in the school-the Chemistry lab,Biology lab & the Computer lab.As the names suggest the labs are used as per specific subjects taught in the school.The labs are located in the forth and fifth floors of the school.

Field

The vast field in front of the school is considered a part of the campus as it is used by the school for programs,sports & physical activity classes.It is a huge field covered with grass.This is where most of the Sports Day outdoor programs and rehersals are held.

school events

Craft Exhibition
Craft Exhibition

Every year at the end of each school year a craft exhibition is held in the school to display all the projects made by the students of every class during school year.Parents visit the school along with their children to see the art & craft made by the students,as well as few skits,dancing and singing performances in the school auditorium.

Sports Day
Sports Day

In the month of December,the school organizes a Sports day program for the students.The preparation for this program takes at least three weeks and is a huge part of the school year.The topic of the program varies and is alternatively set in either a outside field & an indoor auditorium.Parents also come to view this huge ceremony.



Curriculum

  • Academics

    The school follows the ICSE curriculum with English as the medium of instruction and Bengali and Hindi are taught as second languages. The school follows a thematic approach from Nursery to class X through group learning experience, exploration, discovery and experimentation. The school develops basic skills of communication through speaking, writing, reading, calculating and creative art. It encourages scientific and methodical learning through observation, classification, estimation, analytical reasoning and cause-effect relationships. Social skills of wholesome interaction with their peers are inculcated through co-operation, sharing and mutual respect.

  • Extracurricular Activities

    Extracurricular activities include Indian classical dance, music, art, debate, drama, games, quiz, and physical training. Value education and lessons on moral conduct are an integral part of the school’s curriculum.
    Students are offered opportunities to participate in Leadership Training Service, Guides and Bulbuls, Young Christian Students, story club, dramatics club, nature club, quiz and debate club, art & craft club and ‘go as you like’ club.
    Sports include athletics, basketball, volleyball, badminton, tennis, carrom and chess.

Parts of the school symbol

The Crown

The Crown

The crown symbolizes a reward for excellence.

The Laurel Branches

The Laurel Branches

The palm or laurel branches symbolise victory. In ancient times the winner of a race was given a laurel wreath in recognition of their victory;

The Sheild

The Sheild

The shield signifies protection. The training and ideals imparted to us during our school life will act as armour and be a source of strength in our future lives.

The RNDM Symbol

The RNDM Symbol

The RNDM emblem stands for – Religieuses de Notre Dame de Missions (translated, Sisters of Our Lady of the Missions).
This emblem worn by the Sisters of Our Lady of the Missions was designed to celebrate the presence of God among all people across the world. This simple design was chosen in 1974 taking into account that some missions are established where the majority of the people are not Christian.
